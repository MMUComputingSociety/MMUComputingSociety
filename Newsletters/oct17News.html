Welcome

First of all I want to give a huge thank you to all the people who came to see us at the Freshers Fair at the Union during welcome week. Over 60 of you signed up, and we hope to see you all at our events this year. This is the first edition of our newsletter which we will be sending out to you monthly so you know what is happening and how you can get involved.

Do you have a suggestion for an event or a workshop? Send us an email at mmucomputingsoc@gmail.com or speak to us at any of the events below. Let us know what type of events you would like us to offer, or if you would like to run a workshop yourself and if we can put them on, we will.


What are we doing in October?

Unreal Tournament

This Wednesday 4 October we are holding an Unreal Tournament Event at C0.13b in the John Dalton building from 1pm. This is a great opportunity to meet some other members if the society while playing some Unreal Tournament. If you have not played Unreal Tournament from Epic Games before you can download and play it for free here. As this is not a standard campus application we will show you how to install it from our folder in the Staff2Student drive on the university machines on Wednesday.




Introduction to HTML/CSS

On wednesday 11 October we will be running a very simple into to HTML/CSS workshop in C0.13b in the John Dalton building. The workshop will last about 50 minuts and we will cover the basics of creating a simle portfolio page for yourself and stying it with CSS. Some of you will be doing this during the year in your Introduction to Web Design unit, however we are providing this workshop primarily for students who are not taking this unit. Everyone is still welcome to attend however, even if you are taking the web design unit. A portfolio website is a simple to create but powerful tool to showcase your work to potential employers in the future.




Building a Twitter bot with Node JS

On Wednesday 25 October Sean O'Mahoney will be running a workshop showing you how to buuld a Twitter bot in Node JS. The room for this is still to be confirmed but is likely to be in C0.13a/a or c0.17. We will update this page as soon as we have the room booked. This workshop is a great opportunity to get hands on with Node JS and create somthing useful and fun while you are at it. Node.js is a platform built on Chrome's JavaScript runtime for easily building fast and scalable network applications using an event-driven, non-blocking I/O model. If you want to check out Node JS before the lecture you can visit their website here. What is MMU doing in October?

Meet the Spotify Engineer

Manchester Met alumni, Bilawal Hameed, is now working in his dream job as a Developer Advocate Engineer at Spotify Global Headquarters in Sweden. Take this opportunity to hear how his studies at Manchester Metropolitan led him to achieve his life goals, and how you can start building your future now!

How I Got My Dream Job With Spotify - 15:00 - 16:00
Bilawal will share how he achieved two of his biggest life goals: travelling the world and working for a top-tier company like Spotify.

How to Build With Spotify APSs and SDKs - 17.00 - 18.00
Bilawal Hameed is a Developer Advocate Engineer at Spotify. In this talk, he will demonstrate in a beginner-friendly way how to use Spotify's Developer Tools to build an project that interacts with Spotify.

Admission: Free
